# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive and accessible user experiences by designing user flows, wireframes, and interaction patterns. They bridge the gap between users and the product team, ensuring usability is central to every feature.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, and interaction patterns
- Work closely with Product Manager and Developers to ensure usability and accessibility
- Review product changes for UX impact before release
- Communicate user research insights to the team

### Goals
- Create intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings presentations
- Collaboration with PMs on feature specifications
- Design handoff documentation for developers

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define quality standards and testing strategies to ensure high-quality releases. They coordinate testing efforts, track quality metrics, and work closely with developers to establish clear acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and testing strategies for each release
- Coordinate manual and automated testing
- Work with Developers to clarify acceptance criteria and testability
- Track and communicate quality metrics and defects

### Goals
- Ensure high quality releases with minimal defects
- Establish clear quality gates and test coverage
- Enable fast, confident deployments

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews and QA status reports
- Bug triage meetings
- Acceptance criteria clarification with developers
- Quality metrics dashboards and reports

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers develop and maintain comprehensive documentation for users and developers. They partner with cross-functional teams to ensure all features and processes are clearly documented and accessible.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain user-facing and developer documentation
- Partner with PMs and Developers to ensure coverage of new features and process changes
- Maintain docs as a living artifact, supporting onboarding and knowledge transfer
- Review documentation for accuracy, clarity, and completeness

### Goals
- Provide comprehensive, accurate documentation
- Reduce support burden through self-service docs
- Accelerate onboarding and knowledge sharing

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with engineering and product
- Release notes coordination
- Doc feedback loops with users and support
- Content planning and information architecture discussions

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan, coordinate, and communicate releases across teams. They ensure deployment readiness, manage the release calendar, and coordinate dependencies to enable smooth, low-risk releases.

### Responsibilities
- Plan, coordinate, and communicate releases across teams
- Ensure readiness for deployment, rollback, and incident processes
- Work with all other roles to align on timing and status
- Manage release calendar and dependencies

### Goals
- Execute smooth, low-risk releases
- Maintain clear visibility into release status
- Coordinate cross-team dependencies effectively

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and readiness meetings
- Go/no-go decision discussions
- Release announcements and stakeholder updates
- Post-release retrospectives
